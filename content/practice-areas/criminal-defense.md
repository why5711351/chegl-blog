## Your Constitutional Rights

When facing criminal charges, you have important rights:

### Fourth Amendment - Protection from Unreasonable Search and Seizure
Evidence obtained illegally may be suppressed.

### Fifth Amendment - Right Against Self-Incrimination
You have the right to remain silent. Use it.

### Sixth Amendment - Right to Counsel
You have the right to an attorney. Don't speak without one.

### Eighth Amendment - Protection from Excessive Bail
Reasonable bail should be set based on circumstances.

## The Criminal Justice Process

### 1. Arrest
Taken into custody. Booking process. Bail hearing.

### 2. Arraignment
Formal reading of charges. Enter plea (guilty, not guilty, no contest).

### 3. Pre-Trial Motions
Suppress evidence, dismiss charges, change venue.

### 4. Discovery
Exchange of evidence between prosecution and defense.

### 5. Plea Negotiations
Discuss potential plea agreements with prosecutor.

### 6. Trial
Present defense, cross-examine witnesses, argue to jury.

### 7. Sentencing
If convicted, judge determines punishment.

### 8. Appeals
Challenge conviction or sentence.
