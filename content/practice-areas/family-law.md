## Divorce Process Overview

### 1. Initial Consultation
Discuss your situation, goals, and legal options. Understand your rights and realistic outcomes.

### 2. Filing the Petition
Prepare and file divorce papers. Serve your spouse according to state requirements.

### 3. Temporary Orders
Request temporary support, custody, and restraining orders if needed.

### 4. Discovery
Exchange financial information, assets, debts, and other relevant documents.

### 5. Negotiation & Mediation
Attempt to reach settlement agreements on all issues.

### 6. Trial (if necessary)
Present your case to a judge who makes final decisions.

### 7. Final Judgment
Court issues final divorce decree with all terms.

## Child Custody Factors

Courts consider the **best interests of the child**:

- Parent-child relationship
- Parent's ability to provide care
- Child's adjustment to home, school, community
- Mental and physical health of all parties
- Child's preferences (age-dependent)
- History of domestic violence or substance abuse
- Willingness to facilitate relationship with other parent

## Property Division

**Equitable Distribution vs. Community Property:**

### Equitable Distribution States
Assets divided "fairly" (not necessarily equally):
- New York
- New Jersey
- Pennsylvania
- Ohio
- Most East Coast states

### Community Property States
Assets split 50/50:
- California
- Texas
- Arizona
- Nevada
- Washington

## Alimony/Spousal Support

Types of alimony:

**Temporary Alimony** - During divorce proceedings  
**Rehabilitative Alimony** - Support while gaining education/training  
**Permanent Alimony** - Long-term support (less common now)  
**Lump-Sum Alimony** - One-time payment  

Factors considered:
- Length of marriage
- Standard of living during marriage
- Age and health of parties
- Earning capacity
- Contributions to marriage
- Fault (in some states)

## Alternatives to Litigation

### Mediation
Neutral third party helps reach agreement. Less expensive, faster, more control over outcomes.

### Collaborative Divorce
Both parties and attorneys commit to settlement without court. Team approach with financial specialists.

### Arbitration
Private judge makes binding decisions. Faster than court, more flexible scheduling.

## Protecting Your Children

Divorce is hard on children. Minimize impact:

✅ Don't badmouth the other parent  
✅ Maintain routines  
✅ Reassure them it's not their fault  
✅ Keep them out of conflicts  
✅ Consider counseling  
✅ Put their needs first
