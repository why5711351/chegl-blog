# Google AdSense Approval Checklist: Get Approved in 2026

Getting Google AdSense approval can be challenging, but with the right preparation, you can significantly increase your chances. This comprehensive checklist covers everything you need to know.

## AdSense Requirements Overview

Google AdSense has specific requirements that your website must meet before approval. Let's break them down.

## ✅ Pre-Application Checklist

### 1. Website Age and Domain

- **Domain Age**: At least 1-3 months old (recommended)
- **Custom Domain**: Use a professional domain (not free subdomains)
- **DNS Setup**: Properly configured with SSL certificate

**Tip**: New domains can get approved, but older domains with history have better chances.

### 2. Content Requirements

#### Content Quantity
- **Minimum**: 20-30 high-quality articles
- **Word Count**: Each article should be 800+ words
- **Original**: 100% unique content (no copying)

#### Content Quality
- ✅ Well-researched and informative
- ✅ Proper grammar and spelling
- ✅ Clear structure with headings
- ✅ Images with alt text
- ✅ No AI-generated content without editing

#### Content Types That Work Well
- Tutorials and how-to guides
- Product reviews (honest and detailed)
- Industry news and analysis
- Case studies
- List posts (e.g., "Top 10 Tools...")

#### Content to Avoid
- ❌ Copyrighted material
- ❌ Adult content
- ❌ Gambling-related content
- ❌ Hacking/cracking tutorials
- ❌ Medical advice (without credentials)
- ❌ Get-rich-quick schemes

### 3. Required Pages

These pages are **mandatory** for AdSense approval:

#### About Page
- Who you are
- What your website is about
- Your mission/expertise
- Professional photo (optional but recommended)

#### Contact Page
- Email address or contact form
- Physical address (optional but adds credibility)
- Social media links

#### Privacy Policy
- How you collect and use data
- Cookie usage disclosure
- Third-party services (Analytics, AdSense)
- User rights (GDPR/CCPA compliance)
- **Must be accessible from every page**

#### Terms of Service
- User guidelines
- Content usage rights
- Disclaimer information
- Limitation of liability

#### Disclaimer (Recommended)
- Affiliate disclosure
- Accuracy disclaimer
- Professional advice disclaimer

### 4. Website Design and Navigation

#### Design Requirements
- ✅ Clean, professional design
- ✅ Easy to read (good contrast)
- ✅ Mobile-friendly (responsive)
- ✅ Fast loading speed
- ✅ No broken links

#### Navigation
- ✅ Clear menu structure
- ✅ Categories and tags organized
- ✅ Search functionality
- ✅ Breadcrumbs (helpful for UX)
- ✅ Internal linking between articles

### 5. Technical Requirements

#### SSL Certificate
- HTTPS must be enabled
- No mixed content warnings
- Valid SSL certificate

#### Site Speed
- Page load time under 3 seconds
- Optimized images
- Minified CSS/JS
- Good Core Web Vitals scores

#### Mobile Optimization
- Responsive design
- Readable text on mobile
- Touch-friendly buttons
- No horizontal scrolling

**Test your site**: [Google Mobile-Friendly Test](https://search.google.com/test/mobile-friendly)

### 6. Traffic Requirements

While Google doesn't specify minimum traffic:
- **Recommended**: At least 100+ daily visitors
- **Traffic Source**: Organic search traffic preferred
- **Engagement**: Low bounce rate, good time on site

**Note**: Quality over quantity. 100 engaged visitors is better than 1000 bounced visitors.

### 7. SEO Basics

Before applying, ensure:
- ✅ XML sitemap submitted to Google Search Console
- ✅ Robots.txt properly configured
- ✅ Meta titles and descriptions for all pages
- ✅ Proper heading structure (H1, H2, H3)
- ✅ Image alt attributes
- ✅ Internal linking strategy
- ✅ No duplicate content

## ❌ Common AdSense Rejection Reasons

### 1. "Low Value Content"

**Why**: Content is thin, copied, or not useful

**Fix**:
- Write comprehensive, original articles
- Add unique insights and experiences
- Include images, videos, infographics
- Update old content regularly

### 2. "Site Not Ready"

**Why**: Website looks incomplete or unprofessional

**Fix**:
- Add all required pages
- Fix broken links
- Improve design and navigation
- Add more content (aim for 30+ articles)

### 3. "Policy Violation"

**Why**: Content violates AdSense policies

**Fix**:
- Review [AdSense Program Policies](https://support.google.com/adsense/answer/48182)
- Remove prohibited content
- Add proper disclaimers
- Ensure copyright compliance

### 4. "Insufficient Content"

**Why**: Not enough content for ads to be relevant

**Fix**:
- Publish more high-quality articles
- Cover multiple topics within your niche
- Ensure each page has substantial content

### 5. "Navigation Issues"

**Why**: Poor site structure or broken navigation

**Fix**:
- Create clear menu structure
- Fix all broken links
- Add search functionality
- Improve internal linking

## 📋 Application Process

### Step 1: Prepare Your Site

Complete all items in the checklist above.

### Step 2: Create AdSense Account

1. Go to [Google AdSense](https://www.google.com/adsense)
2. Click "Get Started"
3. Sign in with Google account
4. Enter your website URL
5. Select your language
6. Accept terms and conditions

### Step 3: Add AdSense Code

1. After signup, you'll get a code snippet
2. Add it to your website's `<head>` section
3. Verify installation

**For WordPress**: Use a plugin like "Insert Headers and Footers"

**For Hugo/Static Sites**: Add to your base template

### Step 4: Wait for Review

- **Timeline**: Usually 2 days to 2 weeks
- **Status**: Check your AdSense dashboard
- **Email**: You'll receive approval/rejection email

### Step 5: If Rejected

1. Read the rejection reason carefully
2. Fix the identified issues
3. Wait at least 1 week before reapplying
4. Reapply with improved site

## 🚀 Tips for Faster Approval

### Content Strategy
1. **Focus on one niche** - Don't be too broad
2. **Write evergreen content** - Content that stays relevant
3. **Update regularly** - Show active maintenance
4. **Add multimedia** - Images, videos, infographics

### Technical Optimization
1. **Use a professional theme** - Avoid default templates
2. **Optimize site speed** - Use tools like PageSpeed Insights
3. **Fix all errors** - No 404s, no broken images
4. **Secure your site** - HTTPS is mandatory

### User Experience
1. **Clear navigation** - Users should find content easily
2. **Readable content** - Good fonts, spacing, contrast
3. **Mobile-first** - Most traffic is mobile
4. **Engaging design** - Keep visitors on your site

## After Approval: Best Practices

### 1. Ad Placement
- Above the fold (visible without scrolling)
- Within content (native ads perform well)
- Sidebar (for desktop)
- End of articles

### 2. Ad Types to Enable
- Display ads (banner)
- In-article ads
- In-feed ads
- Auto ads (easy setup)

### 3. Compliance
- Don't click your own ads (will get banned!)
- Don't encourage clicks
- Follow AdSense policies
- Monitor performance regularly

### 4. Optimize Earnings
- Test different ad placements
- Enable auto ads for optimization
- Focus on high-CPC niches
- Increase traffic through SEO

## Timeline Expectations

```
Week 1-2:   Domain setup and design
Week 3-6:   Create 20-30 quality articles
Week 7-8:   SEO optimization and traffic building
Week 9:     Apply for AdSense
Week 10-11: Review period
Week 12+:   Ads live, start earning
```

**Note**: This is a realistic timeline. Rushing the process often leads to rejection.

## Conclusion

Getting AdSense approval requires preparation, quality content, and patience. Follow this checklist, focus on providing value to your visitors, and approval will follow.

Remember: AdSense is just one monetization method. Consider affiliate marketing, sponsored content, and digital products as additional revenue streams.
